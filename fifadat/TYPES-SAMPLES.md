##  extra-time or not? about going directly to penalty shootouts

Usually extra time has been played first, but this is not necessary;
exceptions include
- the Copa Libertadores,
- Copa América (quarter-finals, semi-finals, and third-place game),
- CONCACAF Gold Cup (quarter-finals and semi-finals),
- FA Community Shield,
- the EFL League Cup, and
- the Football League Trophy,
all of which use shoot-outs straight after the end of normal time.



Club TournamentsEFL Cup / Carabao Cup (England): Skips extra time from the first round up until the semi-finals. Only the final uses a 30-minute extra-time period if tied.

Copa Libertadores (South America): All knockout matches from the Round of 16 through the semi-finals go straight to penalties if tied on aggregate.
The final is the sole exception that allows extra time.

Copa Sudamericana (South America): Operates identically to the Copa Libertadores, utilizing direct penalties for all knockout rounds except the final.

FA Community Shield (England): The annual curtain-raiser match between the Premier League champion and the FA Cup winner bypasses extra time entirely.

EFL Trophy (England): Tied group-stage matches and knockout rounds go straight to spot-kicks. In the group stage, a penalty winner receives an extra bonus point.

Trophée des Champions (France): The French super cup match goes straight to a shootout if the score is tied after 90 minutes.


---

International Tournaments

Copa América: Bypasses extra time in the quarter-finals, semi-finals, and the third-place playoff match. Similar to South American club competitions, extra time is reserved exclusively for the tournament final.

CONCACAF Gold Cup: Employs a direct penalty shootout rule exclusively for the quarter-final and semi-final stages if teams are level after 90 minutes.
