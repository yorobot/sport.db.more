## trivia - how many (football) countries?


International football outside of FIFA is a vibrant, chaotic ecosystem consisting of nearly 100 "irregular" national and selection teams. These teams represent fully independent nations, unrecognized states, autonomous islands, stateless ethnic minorities, and isolated regions. [1, 2, 3]
Because they cannot or choose not to join FIFA, they have built their own parallel world governing bodies, continental tournaments, and international rivalries. [2, 3, 4]

------------------------------

## 🏛️ The Non-FIFA Organizing Bodies
When teams cannot enter the standard World Cup, they generally operate under one of three systems: [5]

* CONIFA (The Global Alternative): The [Confederation of Independent Football Associations (CONIFA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confederation_of_Independent_Football_Associations) is the dominant force in non-FIFA soccer. It functions as a parallel FIFA for isolated regions and minorities, boasting nearly 60 member teams globally. They host their own highly competitive CONIFA World Football Cup.
* The Island Games Association: An Olympic-style multi-sport event held every two years for autonomous islands (like the Isle of Man, Jersey, and Gotland). Their football tournament is famous for its fierce, historic island rivalries.
* Continental Confederations (The In-Between): A few territories are accepted into a local continental body (like CONCACAF or Oceania) but are not full members of FIFA itself. [2, 6, 7, 8, 9]

------------------------------

## 🗺️ Categories of "Irregular" National Teams
Non-FIFA national teams generally fall into four distinct geopolitical categories:

## 1. Independent Sovereign States Outside FIFA [3]

There are 8 UN-recognized independent nations that are not affiliated with FIFA, mostly due to a lack of professional stadiums, funding, or geographic isolation: [3, 8, 10, 11]

* Monaco & Vatican City: Both have historic national selections that play occasional international friendlies.
* Pacific Island Nations: Kiribati, Tuvalu, Micronesia, Palau, Nauru, and the Marshall Islands. Kiribati and Tuvalu are associate members of the Oceania Football Confederation but are frozen out of FIFA due to substandard facility infrastructure. [7, 8, 10, 11, 12]

## 2. The "In-Between" Regional Members

These are dependent overseas territories that are not independent countries but are officially allowed to play in major regional tournaments (like the Gold Cup), though they are blocked from playing in FIFA World Cup qualifiers: [3, 9]

* The French Overseas Departments: French Guiana, Guadeloupe, Martinique, Saint Martin, and Réunion. These teams feature elite professional players from the French domestic leagues but cannot join FIFA because they are legally a part of France.
* Others: Bonaire, Sint Maarten, Zanzibar, and the Northern Mariana Islands. [3, 9, 13]


## 3. Unrecognized States and De Facto Republics [3]

These are self-governing regions with their own infrastructure, populations, and borders, but they lack widespread United Nations recognition: [2, 14]

* Northern Cyprus & Abkhazia: Two powerhouse teams in the non-FIFA world with highly developed regional leagues.
* Somaliland, Transnistria, and Western Sahara. [3, 6, 7, 14, 15]


## 4. Cultural Regions, Stateless Peoples, and Diaspora Teams [14]

These selections represent distinct cultural, historical, or ethnic groups rather than geographical governments: [2]

* Sápmi: Representing the indigenous Sámi people spanning northern Norway, Sweden, and Finland.
* Chameria & Padania: Cultural and historical regions within Europe.
* Cascadia: A team representing the distinct cultural region of the Pacific Northwest (US/Canada).
* United Koreans in Japan: A selection composed of the historic Korean diaspora community living in Japan. [3, 16]


------------------------------

## ⚽ The Main Non-FIFA Competitions
Non-FIFA teams do not just play friendlies; they compete for major international trophies: [2, 3]

| Tournament [2, 3, 6, 7, 17] | Organized By | Who Competes? |
|---|---|---|
| CONIFA World Football Cup | CONIFA | Global qualifiers for unrecognized states, autonomous regions, and minorities. |
| CONIFA European Football Cup | CONIFA Europe | Highly competitive regional cup (e.g., featuring Greenland and Northern Cyprus in the 2026 edition[](https://www.conifa.org/en/event/conifa-euro-2026/)). |
| Island Games Football | IGA | Autonomous sub-national islands like Shetland, Guernsey[](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0366c), and Greenland. |
| Europeada | FUEN | A specialized tournament specifically for indigenous and linguistic European minorities. |

## 🚀 The Ultimate Goal: Stepping Up to FIFA

For many of these territories, the "irregular" football circuit is a stepping stone to global recognition. Teams like Kosovo and Gibraltar
 spent years playing in non-FIFA matches before successfully battling legal roadblocks to earn official FIFA membership. [Greenland](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/035v3) is currently in the middle of an active, highly-publicized push to move from the non-FIFA circuit into CONCACAF and FIFA. [2, 3, 7]
